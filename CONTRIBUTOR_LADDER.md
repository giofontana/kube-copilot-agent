# Contributor Ladder

This document outlines the path from first-time contributor to project maintainer in KubeCopilot. It is inspired by the [CNCF contributor ladder template](https://contribute.cncf.io/projects/best-practices/templates/).

## Levels

### 1. Contributor

**Who**: Anyone who contributes to the project in any way.

**How to become one**: Submit a PR, file an issue, improve documentation, or participate in discussions.

**Privileges**:
- Listed in GitHub contributors
- Can submit PRs and issues

### 2. Reviewer

**Who**: Contributors who have demonstrated sustained, quality contributions.

**Requirements**:
- At least 3 merged PRs demonstrating understanding of the codebase
- Consistent, constructive participation in reviews and discussions
- Nominated by a maintainer

**Privileges**:
- Added to the GitHub team with triage permissions
- Can approve (but not merge) PRs
- Invited to design discussions

### 3. Maintainer

**Who**: Reviewers who have demonstrated deep project expertise and sustained commitment.

**Requirements**:
- At least 6 months of active contribution as a reviewer
- Deep understanding of at least one major component (operator, agent server, Web UI, Helm charts)
- Demonstrated good judgment in code review
- Nominated by a maintainer, approved by maintainer consensus

**Privileges**:
- Full write access to the repository
- Can merge PRs and cut releases
- Voting rights on governance decisions
- Listed in [MAINTAINERS.md](MAINTAINERS.md)

## Stepping Down

Contributors at any level may step down at any time. Maintainers who step down are moved to the Emeritus list. There is no shame in stepping down — life circumstances change, and we appreciate all contributions.

## Inactivity

- **Reviewers**: After 3 months of inactivity, reviewer status may be revoked (can be restored upon return)
- **Maintainers**: After 6 months of inactivity, a discussion will be held about emeritus status
